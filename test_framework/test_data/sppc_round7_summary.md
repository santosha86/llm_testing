## 1. Round 7 Overview

## 2. Grid Integration Analysis

### 2.1 Northern Region Assessment

The Northern Region (Al-Jouf, Northern Border, Tabuk) presents unique challenges:

**Available Grid Capacity: 1,400 MW** (per ECRA regulatory framework)
**Planned Round 7 Allocation: 900 MW**
**Gap Analysis: 500 MW remaining after Round 7**

### 2.2 Integration Concerns

1. Transmission line upgrades required for Northern projects
2. Substation capacity needs assessment
3. Grid stability studies ongoing

---

## 3. Bidder Requirements Update

### 3.1 Enhanced Local Content

For Round 7, local content requirements are increased:
- Minimum: 35% (up from 30% in previous rounds)
- Target: 45% for bonus evaluation points

### 3.2 Technology Specifications

Solar PV projects must use:
- Bifacial modules (minimum)
- Single-axis tracking systems
- Minimum module efficiency: 21%

- Equipment: SAR 8.0 billion

## 5. Risk Assessment

### 5.1 Key Risks

1. **Grid capacity constraints** - Northern region grid may need upgrades
2. **Supply chain delays** - Global equipment shortages
3. **Financing conditions** - Interest rate volatility
4. **Local content achievement** - Limited local manufacturing

### 5.2 Mitigation Measures

- Early engagement with SEC for grid upgrades
- Pre-qualification of multiple equipment suppliers
- Fixed-rate financing requirements in PPA
- LCGPA coordination for local content support
